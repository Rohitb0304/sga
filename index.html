Why Students Underperform in Academic and Competitive Examinations?

Academic and competitive exams are critical milestones in a student’s life, yet many struggle to perform to their full potential. According to Dr. Nilkanth Kesari, a renowned expert in student performance, low performance stems from seven critical factors that hinder progress.

Low Grasping Power

Difficulty in absorbing new concepts quickly hinders students’ ability to understand complex topics in exams.

Low Memory Power

Weak memory retention can lead to forgetting key details during exams, affecting overall performance.

Low Perception Power

A lack of quick perception can delay decision-making and hinder problem-solving during exams.

Low Emotional Stability

Emotional instability can create stress and anxiety, impairing concentration and performance during exams.

Low Intellectual Power

Limited cognitive ability may affect a student’s capacity to apply logic and reasoning during exams.

No Career Goal Clarity

Without a clear vision of their career goals, students may lack the motivation needed to perform well in exams.

Physical Aspects

Physical health issues such as fatigue or poor nutrition can significantly affect a student’s ability to perform in exams.
